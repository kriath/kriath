Resources for Players and DMs


  • CritterDB

    Great way to collate your homebrew monsters, and publish them into a PDF if you wish.

  • Dicecloud

    Dicecloud is a fantastic online character sheet. Has a fairly steep learning curve.

  • DMs Guild

    DMs Guild is a fantastic repository for homebrew content. Contains both paid and free content.

  • donjon

    donjon is a fantastic random generator that can generate random dungeons, encounters, treasure and more. Highly recommended if you need a quick burst of inspiration.

  • GM Binder

    GM Binder is a great way to collate all your homebrew documents. Utilises markdown.

  • Hero Forge

    Hero Forge offers a way to design and order your own custom miniatures. I highly recommend their "Premium Plastic" option if you intend to use the miniatures often as well as paint them. Also offers the STL files if you have a 3D printer yourself. Expensive, but worth it.

  • Homebrewery

    Homebrewery is an alternative to GM Binder and fulfills a similar function. Uses markdown like GM Binder.

  • Inkarnate

    Inkarnate is a fantastic tool for map making. Primarily suited for world maps.

  • Kobold Fight Club

    Kobold Fight Club is a fantastic encounter builder for both old and new DMs alike. Highly recommended.

  • OneNote

    Microsoft's OneNote is a fantastic piece of software. It's a great way to take notes for your adventures. Visit http://www.cryrid.com/digitaldnd/ to see more on how well this can work for you.

  • /r/characterdrawing

    A great subreddit to get artwork done from. Both paid and free artwork.

  • Syrinscape

    Another audio streaming service. Has an app so can easily run from your Android or iOS device. Actually has offical soundpacks licensed from Wizards of the Coast, if you prefer "official" methods.

  • Tabletop Audio

    Website to stream audio from, if you like to use ambient music for your games. Great resource to keep in the back of your mind.

  • Who The Fuck Is My DND Character?

    Title pretty much sums it up. Generates random character concepts. Hilarious.
